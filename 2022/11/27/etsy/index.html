  1. Tell me about a time when you use data to make an impact.
    When evaluating the performance of the providers in each specialty, we need to set a threshold of the minimum volume of the providers to filter those very small providers since the measure rate of those providers are not reliable due to the sample size is too small. Previously the methodology to set the threshold is rely on human decision. The medical directors will check the remaining volume of the providers and members after setting different threshold and pick one they think is reasonable. Since the process is time-consuming and repetitive to have a meeting to talk about the threshold for every specialty we launched. So I developed a metric to measure the performance of the threshold. Then I build a pipeline to directly select the threshold with the maximum metric score as the best threshold. And that influence the team’s decision on choosing the threshold of the minimum volume of the providers. And saved a lot of time to make the decision and make the whole
    process more effective.

The evaluation metric = weighted percentage of the remained providers * threshold / weighted average of the difference of the standard deviation before and after applying the threshold of each metric
(The metric is related with the weighted percentage of the remained providers using the volume of the metric as the weight and weighted difference of the standard deviation before and after applying the threshold and the threshold of the volume. The larger the weighted percentage of the remained providers and the smaller the weighted difference of the standard deviation which indicates a better performance of the threshold.)

  • Customer Lifetime Value
    The Customer Lifetime Value (CLV) is an understanding of the total revenue attributed by the entire relationship (including future purchases) with a customer. The CLV of a customer is calculated based on their average purchase value, and multiplying that number by the average purchasing frequency to give you the customer value. This value is then multiplied by the average customer lifespan to determine the CLV.

Review search queries that led to your listings

  1. Search query: The search query is the exact wording that someone searched on Etsy.com. Shopper may have seen your listings in the search results.
  2. Impressions: How many times shoppers saw your listings for this query.
  3. Position: The average place your listings appear in search results for this query.
  4. Visits: The total number of times shoppers browsed your shop. “Browsing” means that a shopper viewed your listings and shop.
  5. Visits per impression: The percent of times a person saw your listing in search and visited your shop.
  6. Orders: The total number of orders from shoppers who found your shop through this search query.
  7. Conversion rate: The percent of visits from this search query that resulted in a sale.
  8. Revenue: Your gross earnings from shoppers who found your listing through this search query and ordered an item. This is the revenue for the entire order.
  9. Average order value: The average amount paid for orders from shoppers who found your shop through this search query.
  10. Listings: The number of your listings that appeared in the search results for this query.
