  1. ** Translate the business goal into a metric**
  2. Choose a metric: start from high level company goal
    • growth: user acquisition/user engagement/user retention
    • High majority of metrics need a time threshold in order to be evaluated
    • When asked to optimize a long term metric like retention rate or lifetime value,
      the question means: find a short term metric that can predict the long term one, and then focus on optimizing that one

Q1. How would you improve engagement on FB(Etsy)?

  1. Define a concrete metric related to company mission.
  • Define the engagement on FB as the proportition of users who take at least one action per day.
    one action:
  • make a click/put some item in favourite/in the shopping car
  • search one item in search bar
  • complete one transacation
  1. After the metric, pick the variables you think would matter tp move that metric.
  • Always a combination of user characteristics.(gender/age/country) and user behavior(device/where come from ads/Etsy website/search engine)
  1. Pick a model to predict the metric form point 1 using variables from point 2
  • Emphasize why you picked that mode
  1. Come up with a couple of possible model outputs.
  • Try to always have one “good” segment and one “bad”. Use your knowledge of their product to try to come up with realistic segments. Otherwise, just make them up in a way that are actionable and emphasize that these are just examples, with real data you would find the real segments (after all, the job of a DS is to suggest actions based on the data, not guessing in advance what you will find in the data).
  1. Define next steps.
  • Check the translation page
  • try with a more localized version
  • tell the marketing team to reach more of them via ads or specific marketing campaigns

Q2. We are launching a new driver app with a better UI. The goal is increasing driver earnings by increasing their number of trips. Outline a testing strategy to see if the new app is better than the old one.

  • Need to make sure the test and control group are independent.

  • Match comparable markets in pairs.

  • Choose how many markets you need. define in advance sample size needed for a t-test. To identify required sample size, choose power, significance level, minimum difference between test and control, and std deviation. Review how to estimate t-test sample size. It is often asked.

  • Run the test and, after having reached the required sample size, check if results are significant.

  • check for novelty effect. Users use more a product when it is new. Not because it is better, but simply because it is new.
    As novelty ends, they will use it less. This is called novelty effect and often makes tests look like winners when they are not.

    • Solution for novelty: Subset the users into new users and old users. If the test wins in over all but not in new users, then
      it’s flag that there exist novelty effect.
