Product Sense

  1. What are companies looking for?
  • Structure - demonstrate a systematic approach
  • Comprehensiveness - covers all import aspects
  • Feasibility - practical enough that it could be implemented realistically

Three Categories of product sense questions

  1. Diagnose a problem
    You are given a scenario that one of the import metrics has shifted to the negative direction and you are asked to
    figure out the root cause of the issue.

Framework of Diagnose a problem

  1. Clarify the scenario/metric
  2. Time - sudden or stable?
  • Internal - Data source? Data collection? Bug?
  • External - Seasonality? Industry trend? Competitors? Special event? Natural disater?
  1. Other product/feature by the same company?
  • Have we made any change to our product line?
  • Have other related products experienced the same change?
  1. Segment by user demographic and behavioral features
  • Regions
  • Languages
  • Platforms - IOS/Android/Web
  1. Decompose the metric(optional)
  • Daily Active User(DAU) = Existing users + New users + Resurrected users - Churned users
  1. Summarize overall approach
  • Most reasonable cuases
  • How to fix
  1. Measure Success
    You are asked how to measure the success/health of a product/feature
  2. Clarify function and goal of the product/feature
  • What it does? How is it used? Who is it for?
  • Huge red flag if you start answering without clarification!
  1. Define metrics(no more than 3)

  • 2 Success metrics to measure the success

    • DAU
    • number of bookings
    • conversion rate

  • 1 Guardrail metric
    Should not degrade in pursuit of a new product/feature

    • cancelation rate
    • bounce rate

  • Good metrics should fit the context

    • For example: measure the recommendation model
    • click through rate
    • percentage of users who actually purchased the item
    • Guardrail metric: average time to return the result(good & quick)
  1. Launch OR NOT
    You are asked how to test a product idea or whether to launch a product/feature
    Always need to make a recommendation on whether launch the product or not!

Framework

  1. Clarify goal and define the metrics
  • Similar to Measure Success
  1. Experimentation
  • How to design it?
  • How to split the users?
  • How long to run it?
  1. Recommendation Based on Experiment Results
  • 3.1 Link results to the goal and business impact
  • eg. what does 0.01% lift in conversion rate translate to revenue? Is it worthy to lauch the product given the cost?
  • 3.2 Conflicting results
  • increase in DAU but increase in bounce rate
  • Translate that to the impact to the users and business
  • 3.3 Short-term vs. long-term
  • launch brings in more users to the platform thus benefits may outweigh drawbacks.

Before the interview

  • Research the company
  • deeper and better conversation

During the Interview

  • Always clarify the question
    • High-level goal
  • Listen to the feedback, don’t follow frameworks rigidly
  • Write down the thought process with bullet points

Tips:

  1. Interact with the interviewer
  • Design an experiment
    I can think of selecting the right metric, obtaining the minimal detectable effect, choosing the randomization unit,
    calculating the sample size. Is there anything you want me to talk about specifically?
  1. Always clarify the questions
    CTR
  • CTR = # of clicks/ # of impressions
  • CTR = # of unique users clicked/# of unique users visited the page

What is the goal of a feature or a product?
How does a particular feature work?
What data do we have to support a finding?

My understanding of the goal of this new feature is two folds:
one is to increase engagement on the platform
and the other one is to drive long term revrenue
Is my understanding correct?

  1. Ask time to structure your communication
  2. It’s okay to make mistakes
  3. Quality is better than quantity

Red falg:

  1. No ideas.
  2. Too many ideas. Depth is more important than broadth
  3. Can not defend yourself
  4. Follow the framework blindly.
